Our Team

Team Autocycle is a research team from the University of Maryland's Gemstone Research Honors Program consisting of 14 undergraduate students working on developing semi-autonomous electric bicycle to improve bikeshare systems. Our chosen majors span the gamut from Aerospace Engineering to Art, and we are dedicated to using our research to Do Good in the world around us.

Team Autocycle. From left to right, back row: Joseph O'Leary, Cooper Grill, Michael Allen, Evan Ruderman, Jacob Bartolomei, Andoni Sanguesa, Mikhail Khrenov. Front row: Jack Mirenzi, Mei Blatchford, Isaac Rose, Logan Swaisgood, Meilina Amaral, Zev Traum, Jeremy Carter.

Our Mission

Bikeshare schemes currently have one glaring problem: getting to the bike. Whether at one of several hubs, or lying on a street corner, the fact that any user has to go to the transportation method negates many of its advantages, especially in rarely traversed and underserved areas. We have a solution: have the bike come to you!

Research Questions

In order to accomplish this goal we will be working to expand upon existing research in bicycle actuation, stability, autonomy, and navigation to create a holistic system for our purposes. In doing so we seek to answer the following questions:

  • What are the most effective methods for integrating controls and actuation into a pre-existing bicycle to implement autonomous capabilities in an urban setting?
  • How can these controls methods be optimally combined with navigation technology to fully implement a single autonomous bicycle that could be utilized in a larger bike-sharing system?
  • How could this proposed technology be applied to not only moving people but also the transportation of goods while also minimizing changes to the overarching design?
